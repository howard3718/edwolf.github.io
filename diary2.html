Deep Clean

Dust Plague of the Builders Van

I bought the van from a chippy, and you could very much tell it was a builder's van. It was pretty dusty and tatty (not dirt, more like glue resin and paint/plaster). So the first job I decided to do was strip out the rear ply lining and carpet covering, then remove the headboard and completely clean the lot including the cab.

This photo with the dog is the only one I have of the old carpet lining in the back before. It was very good for the most part and wouldn't have needed changing if it was just a work van, but nowehere near what I wanted for a camper (the seats weren't there originally, I just placed them there to make sure they fit).

At first, I was planning on just removing a few panels to make the cleaning easier, however every panel/trim I seemed to remove revealed a copious amount more building dust. This made me go further and further, until I had completely removed the entire front dash, and even took out the seats and foot well covers, with the rubber flooring being the only thing left which I lifted in place to clean underneath. It became about a 5 day process however I realised that if I didn’t do it the dust would just keep coming back and cause more problems, and that it would be complete peace of mind knowing you could metaphorically eat off any surface.

The result was a very peculiar looking van, as the only thing left was the steering column and bulkhead, which makes a vehicle look very weird and makes you realise how everything we interact with is just a complete façade to make us think we know what’s going on.

However, after 5 days the clean was complete and the van mostly back together again and working. The one thing I didn't do till I was also doing the panel repair was the rear bumper, which had been covered in a plaster/mastic spill, and so had to remove it to clean it as well as for the panel repair. When I did take it off though I had to replace a reversing sensor connection and bend the bumper-subframe straight, as it had evidently been bent at one point and had cut the sensor wire at the same time.
